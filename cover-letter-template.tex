\makelettertitle

I am applying for the postdoctoral position working on \emph{Integrating causal evolutionary processes into phylogenetic comparative methods} (CEPCM). I believe my expertise, work ethic and research interests make me an ideal candidate for this position. In particular, my research focuses on understanding the patterns and dynamics of ecological communities through the lens of coevolutionary theory. My doctoral dissertation, which I successfully defended on June 30, 2020, contributes to this pursuit by providing a mechanistic derivation of a holistic framework capturing the stochastic eco-evolutionary dynamics of ecological communities over microevolutionary time scales. However, the majority of biological diversity has evolved and is maintained through macroevolutionary processes occurring over vast spans of time. Hence, my interests have turned to understanding how models of macroevolutionary dynamics can be formally derived from mechanistic microevolutionary models. Although this objective is in alignment with CEPCM, I understand the goals of CEPCM are much broader than this. Following information provided by \emph{\href{https://grantome.com/grant/NSF/DEB-1942717}{grantome.com}}, the goals of CEPCM include the integration of microevolutionary data into phylogenetic comparative methods, connecting macroevolutionary models with the field of causal inference and the development of biology curricula that aids students in making the non-intuitive shift from Mendelian genetics to macroevolution. Working to accomplish these goals of CEPCM will broaden my scope of scientific expertise and contribute to my development as an educator. By hiring me as a postdoctoral researcher, you will gain a very enthusiastic, curious and productive new lab member who is dedicated to maintaining an inclusive and safe space for all. Taken together, I believe this position provides the ideal next step in my career as a scientist. I hope that you agree.
