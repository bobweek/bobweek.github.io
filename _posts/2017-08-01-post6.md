I am currently developing a likelihood based framework for inferring the strength of coevolution in natural populations from phenotypic data. The method is based on classic quantitative genetic models of coevolution developed primarily by Scott Nuismer. The basic idea is to derive a bivariate distribution of trait means across a metapopulation for two interacting species. At equilibrium, this approach allows us to write a likelihood in terms of coevolutionary selection parameters. We then numerically solve for the maximum likelihood estimates of these parameters and use the likelihood framework to test for the significance of these parameters. If both parameters are statistically distinguishable from zero, then our method provides evidence for the existence of underlying coevolution.
