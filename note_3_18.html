The fast Fourier transform

Fix \(N\). Given \(v=(v(0),\dots,v(N-1))\in\mathbb{C}^N\). The DFT of \(v\) is

\[\hat v(m)=\frac{1}{\sqrt N}\sum_n v(n)e_n(i).\]

Set \(\omega=e^{\frac{2\pi i}{N}}\). Then

\[\hat v(m)=\frac{1}{\sqrt N}\sum_n v(n)\omega^{-mn}.\]

It takes at most \(N-2\) multiplications to compute \(\omega^2,\dots,\omega^{N-1}\).

For each \(\hat v(m)\), there are \(N+1\) multiplications and \(N-1\) additions.

In total to get \(\hat v\), we need \(N(N+1+N-1)+N-2=2(N^2-1)+N<2N^2+N\).

The FFT reduces the number of operations needed to calculate the DFT.

For the FFT we use vectors of length \(N=2^i\) (powers of two). Otherswise add zeros to \(v\) (zero padding).

\[\hat v(m)=<v,e_m>\]

Idea:

  1. Reduce \(F_N\) to two copies of \(F_{N/2}\).

  2. Iteratively apply this reduction

F_N -> 2(F_{N/2}) -> 4(F_{N/4}) -> -> \(N_{2^j}\)

  1. Stop when \(N/{2^j}=1\implies j=log_2(N)\).

Then separating even and odd terms

\[\hat v(m)=\frac{1}{\sqrt N}\sum_n v(n)e^{-2\pi i mn/N}\]

\[=\sum_{n=0}^{N/2-1}\frac{v(2n) }{\sqrt{n}}e^{-2\pi i m 2n /N}+ \sum_{n=0}^{N/2-1}\frac{v(2n+1)}{\sqrt{n}}e^{-2\pi i m (2n+1)/N}\]

\[=\frac{1 }{\sqrt 2}\sum_{n=0}^{N/2-1}\frac{v(2n) }{\sqrt{N/2}}e^{-2\pi i m 2n /N}+ \frac{e^{-2\pi i m/N}}{\sqrt 2}\sum_{n=0}^{N/2-1}\frac{v(2n+1)}{\sqrt{N/2}}e^{-2\pi i m n/(N/2)}=\star\]

Set \(v_0(n)\equiv v(2n)\) & \(v_1\equiv v(2n+1)\).

\[\star=\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}\left(\hat v_0(m)+e^{-2\pi i m/N}\hat v_1(m)\right)\]

Note that \(\hat v(m+N/2)\) is a linear combination of the same two sums. In particular

\[\hat v(m+N/2)=\sum\frac{v(2n )}{\sqrt N}e^{-2\pi i mn/(N/2)}- e^{-2\pi im/N}\sum\frac{v(2n+1)}{\sqrt N}e^{-2\pi i mn/(N/2)}\]

\[=\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}\left(\hat v_0(m)-e^{-2\pi i m/N}\hat v_1(m)\right)\]

\(\#N\equiv\min\) no. of operations needed to calculate Fourier coefficients in \(\mathbb{C}^N\).

Calculating \(\omega,\omega^2,\dots,\omega^N\) requires no more than \(N\) operations. So

\[\#N\leq N+2*\#(N/2)+3N\]

The \(3N\) comes from the two multiplications and one addition involved with

\[\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}\left(\hat v_0(m)-e^{-2\pi i m/N}\hat v_1(m)\right).\]

Theorem (Steins book)

With \(N=2^n\) it is possible to calculate the Fourier coefficients of vectors in \(\mathbb{C}^N\) with at most

\[4*2^n*n=2Nlog_2 N=O(N log_2 N)\]

Proof:

Using induction and the above relation:

  1. \(n=1\), \(N=2\). Then

\[\hat v(0) = \frac{1}{sqrt 2}(\hat v_0(0)+\hat v_1(0)) = \frac{1}{sqrt 2}(\hat v(0)+\hat v(1))\] \[\hat v(1) = \frac{1}{sqrt 2}(\hat v_0(0)+e^{-i\pi}\hat v_1(0)) = \frac{1}{sqrt 2}(\hat v(0)-\hat v(1))\]

making for up to 5 operations. 5<8 (result holds)

  1. Assume the result holds for up to \(N=2^{n-1}\). Then

\[\#N=\#(2^{n-1})\leq 4*2^{n-1}(n-1).\]

  1. \(\#(2^n)=\#(2*2^{n-1})\leq 2\#(2^{n-1})+42^n\leq 2*4*2^{n-1}+4*2^n\)

\[\#(2^n)\leq4*2^n(n-1)+4*2^n=4*2^nn.\]

\(\square\)
