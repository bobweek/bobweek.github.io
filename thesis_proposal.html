The Coevolutionary Theory of Community Ecology

Pairwise Coevolution

The inference method is a major aspect of this section. However, I still would like to incorporate into my framework diversifying selection, variance dynamics and population dynamics.

Coevolutionary Community Modules

This section is inspired by Bob Holt’s community modules. However, instead of focusing on population dynamics, the focus will initially be on mean trait dynamics. The addition of trait variance dynamics and population dynamics would likely follow.

Diffuse Coevolution

Here I will take a similar approach to the metapopulation model of pairwise coevolution but instead apply it to a large community of organisms, starting with one guild (such as the finches or anolis lizards) and later moving to multiple guilds (such as plant/pollinator communities or trophic webs such as primary producers, herbivores and carnivores).

KL vs \(\Lambda\)

Definition of \(\Lambda\):

\[\Lambda=2\ln{\left( \frac{L_c}{L_0} \right)}\]

where \(L_0\) is the likelihood of the null and \(L_c\) is the likelihood of coevolution. For a single observation \(z=(z_1,z_2)^\top\) this simplifies to

\[\ln{\left( \frac{|\Sigma_0|}{|\Sigma_c|} \right)}+(z-\mu_0)^\top\Sigma_0^{-1}(z-\mu_0)-(z-\mu_c)^\top\Sigma_c^{-1}(z-\mu_c)\]

where \(\mu_i\) and \(\Sigma_i\) are the mean vector and variance/covariance matrix of hypothesis \(i\).

Definition of KL:

\[D_{KL}(\phi_c\|\phi_0)=\int_{{\mathbb{R}}^2}\phi_c(z)\ln{\left( \frac{\phi_c(z)}{\phi_0(z)} \right)}dz\] Since \(\phi_c(z)\) and \(\phi_0(z)\) are equivalent to \(L_c\) and \(L_0\) for the single observation \(z\) we see that

\[2D_{KL}(\phi_c\|\phi_0)={\mathbb{E}}_{\phi_c}(\Lambda).\]

That is, twice the KL-divergence is equal to the expected likelihood ratio statistic given the existence of coevolution. This expectation comes out to be:

\[\ln{\left( \frac{|\Sigma_0|}{|\Sigma_c|} \right)}+(\mu_c-\mu_0)^\top\Sigma_0^{-1}(\mu_c-\mu_0)+{\mathrm{tr}}(\Sigma_0^{-1}\Sigma_c)-2\]
