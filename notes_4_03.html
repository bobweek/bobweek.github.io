Harmonic Analysis 03-29-19

From MRA to wavelets:

Need a family \(\{\psi_{j,k}\equiv2^{j/2}\psi(2^jx-k)\}_{j,k\in\mathbb{Z}}\) that is an ONB of \(L^2(\mathbb{R})\). This comes from an MRA:

\[\dots\subset V_{-1}\subset V_0\subset V_1\subset\dots\]

with a scaling function \(\varphi\) s.t. \(\{\varphi(x-k)\}_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}\) is an ONB of \(V_0\). This means that \(\{\varphi_{j,k}\}_k\) is an ONB of \(V_j\). Furthermore \(V_{j+1}=V_j\oplus W_j\); for each \(\varphi\) there is a corresponding \(\psi\) s.t. \(\{\psi_{j,k}\}_k\) is an ONB of \(W_j\). Considering all possible \(j\); \(L^2(\mathbb{R})=\overline{\bigoplus_j W_j}\) and \(\{\psi_{j,k}\}_{j,k}\) is an ONB of \(L^2(\mathbb{R})\).

Question: what is the relationship between \(\varphi\) and \(\psi\)?

Comment: we may think Fourier analysis is outdated given all this modern wavelet stuff. however, the Fourier transform is essential for constructing wavelets.

By the requirements of \(\varphi\), we have

\[\int_\mathbb{R}\varphi(x-k)\varphi(x-l)dx=\delta_{kl}.\]

This is equivalent to \(\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|\hat\varphi(\gamma+k)|^2=1\) (for any \(\gamma\in\mathbb{R}\)) since

Lemma: Let \(f\in L^2(\mathbb{R})\). The family \(\{f(x-k)\}_k\) is orthonormal iff

\[\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|\hat f(\gamma+n)|^2=1, \ \gamma\in\mathbb{R}\]

Proof: Set \(\tau_k\) the translation by \(k\) operator so that \(\tau_k f=f(x-k)\). By change of variables (and translation invariance of Lebesgue integration)

\[\left<\tau_kf,\tau_mf\right>=\left<\tau_{k-m}f,f\right>\]

The orthonormality of \(\{\tau_kf\}\) is equivalent to

\[\left<\tau_kf,f\right>=\delta_0(k)\]

\[=\left<\mathscr F(\tau_kf),\mathscr F(f)\right>=\int_\mathbb{R}\mathscr{F}(\tau_kf)\overline{\mathscr{F}(f)}d\gamma\]

\[=\int_\mathbb{R}e^{-2\pi ik\gamma}\hat f\overline{\hat f}d\gamma=\int_\mathbb{R}e^{-2\pi i k\gamma}|\hat f|^2d\gamma\]

\[=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}\int_n^{n+1}e^{-2\pi i k\gamma}|\hat f|^2d\gamma=\star\]

Let \(\nu=\gamma-n\) for each interval \((n,n+1)\) in the partition of \(\mathbb{R}\):

\[\star=\sum_n\int_0^1 e^{-2\pi i k(\nu+n)}|\hat f(\nu+n)|^2d\nu\]

\[=\sum_n\int_0^1 e^{-2\pi i k\nu}|\hat f(\nu+n)|^2d\nu\]

\[=\int_0^1e^{-2\pi i k\nu}\sum_n|\hat f(\nu+n)|^2d\nu=\star\]

The function \(F(\nu)\equiv\sum_n|\hat f(\nu+n)|^2\) is 1-periodic and \(\star\) calculates the \(k\)th Fourier coeff of \(F(\nu)\) and is equal to \(\delta_0(k)\).

Let \(h\equiv1\) on \([0,1)\):

\[\hat h(k)=\int_0^1 e^{-2\pi ikt}dt=\left.\frac{e^{-2\pi ikt}}{-2\pi ik}\right|_0^1\]

\[=\frac{1-e^{-2\pi ik}}{2\pi ik}=\delta_0(k)\]

Thus \(F=h\) (ae) by the uniqueness of Fourier transform. \(\square\)

Two important properties the scaling function must hold

  1. \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}|\hat\varphi(\gamma+n)|^2=1\)

  2. there is \(\{h_k\}\) s.t. \(\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|h_k|^2<\infty\) and

\[\varphi(x)=\sqrt 2\sum_kh_k\varphi(2x-k)=\sum_k h_k\varphi_{1,j}\]

This is because \(\varphi\in V_0\subset V_1\) and \(\{\varphi_{1,k}\}\) is an ONB of \(V_1\). Note \(h_k=\left<\varphi,\varphi_{1,k}\right>\).

Take the Fourier transform of \(\varphi\)

\[\hat\varphi(\gamma)=\sum_k h_k\hat\varphi_{1,k}\]

\[\hat\varphi_{1,k}=\sqrt{2}e^{-2\pi ik\gamma/2}\frac{1}{2}\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

\[=\frac{e^{-2\pi ik\gamma/2}}{\sqrt 2}\hat \varphi(\gamma/2)\]

and thus

\[\hat\varphi(\gamma)=\hat \varphi(\gamma/2)H(\gamma/2)\]

where \(H(\gamma)=\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}\sum_kh_ke^{-2\pi ik\gamma}\) is a 1-periodic function, a trigonometric function. This is the transfer function of a low pass filter. This is just property ii. and only requires \(\varphi\in V_1\) and thus holds for any \(f\in V_1\). The following depends also on property i.

Lemma: Given an MRA with scaling function \(\varphi\) and a corresponding filter \(H\):

\[|H(\gamma)|^2+|H(\gamma+1/2)|^2=1, \ \ \ \gamma\in\mathbb{R}.\]

Proof: From i. we have \(1=\sum_n|\varphi(\gamma+n)|^2\) which is

\[=\sum_n|H(\frac{\gamma+n}{2})|^2|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma+n}{2})|^2\]

\[=\sum_{n\text{ odd }}|H(\frac{\gamma+n}{2})|^2|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma+n}{2})|^2+\sum_{n\text{ even }}|H(\frac{\gamma}{2})|^2|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma}{2}+\frac{n}{2})|^2\]

period 1

\[=|H(\frac{\gamma}{2}+\frac{1}{2})|^2\sum_{n\text{ odd}}|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma}{2}+\frac{n}{2})|^2+\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|H(\gamma/2)|^2|\hat\varphi(k+\gamma/2)|^2\]

\[=|H(\frac{\gamma}{2}+\frac{1}{2})|^2\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma+2k+1}{2})|^2+\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|H(\gamma/2)|^2|\hat\varphi(k+\gamma/2)|^2\]

\[=|H(\frac{\gamma}{2}+\frac{1}{2})|^2\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|\hat\varphi(\frac{\gamma+1}{2}+k)|^2+\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}|H(\gamma/2)|^2|\hat\varphi(k+\gamma/2)|^2\]

\[=|H(\gamma/2+1/2)|^2+|H(\gamma/2)|^2 \ \ \square\]

Let \(f\in V_1\), by the previous argument for \(\varphi\)

\[\hat f(\gamma)=m_f(\gamma/2)\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

where \(m_f\) is of period 1.

Lemma: \(f\in W_0\) iff there is \(v(\gamma)\) of period one s.t.

\[\hat f(\gamma)=e^{i\pi\gamma}\overline{H(\gamma/2+1/2)}\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

Proof sketch: \(f\in W_0\) iff \(f\in V_1\) and \(f\in {V_0}^\perp\).

  1. since \(f\in V_1\), we need to show that the filter \(m_f(\gamma)\) satisfies

\[m_f(\gamma)=e^{2\pi i\gamma}\sigma(\gamma)\overline{H(\gamma+1/2)}\]

where \(\sigma\) is a function of period \(1/2\).

  1. \(f\perp V_0\) iff \(\left<f,\varphi(x-k)\right>=0\) for \(k\in\mathbb{Z}\)

\[0=\left<\hat f,\hat \varphi(x-k)\right>=\int_\mathbb{R}\hat f(\gamma)e^{2\pi ik\gamma}\overline{\hat\varphi(\gamma)}d\gamma\]

\[=\int_\mathbb{R}m_f(\gamma/2)\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\overline{H(\gamma/2)} \ \overline{\hat\varphi(\gamma)}d\gamma\]

\[\dots \ \square\]

Want: to find \(\psi\) (the wavelet) s.t. \(\{\psi_{j,k}\}\) is an ONB of \(L^2(\mathbb{R})\). Since \(\psi\in W_0\), we have

\[\hat\psi(\gamma)=m_\psi(\gamma/2)\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

where \(m_\psi(\gamma)=e^{2\pi i \gamma}\sigma(\gamma)\overline{H(\gamma+1/2)}\) where \(\sigma\) has period \(1/2\).

Applying an above lemma to \(m_\psi\) (instead of \(H\)) we have

\[|m_\psi(\gamma)|^2+|m_\psi(\gamma+1/2)|^2=1.\]

That is,

\[|\sigma(\gamma)|^2|H(\gamma+1/2)|^2+|\sigma(\gamma+1/2)|^2|H(\gamma)|^2=1.\]

Then \(\sigma\) must have modulus \(1\). The MRA is given, so \(H\) is determined. Pick \(\sigma\equiv 1\) and define \(\psi\in W_0\) as

\[\hat\psi(\gamma)=m_\psi(\gamma/2)\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

where \(m_\psi(\gamma)=e^{2\pi i \gamma}\overline{H(\gamma+1/2)}\).

\[\hat\psi_{0,k}=e^{-2\pi ik\gamma}m_\psi(\gamma/2)\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

and \(\psi_{0,k}\) is also in \(W_0\) for each \(k\).

Let \(f\in W_0\implies \ \exists \ v(\gamma)\) of period one s.t.

\[\hat f(\gamma)=v(\gamma)e^{\pi i \gamma}\overline{H(\gamma/2+1/2)}\hat\varphi(\gamma/2)\]

\[v(\gamma)=\sum_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}a_ke^{-2\pi ik\gamma}\]

\[\hat f(\gamma)=\sum_k a_ke^{-2\pi ik\gamma}\hat\psi(\gamma)\]

\[\hat f(\gamma)=\sum_k a_k\hat\psi_{0,k}(\gamma)\]

and thus, \(f(x)=\sum_k a_k\psi_{0,k}\) when \(f\in W_0\). If \(f\) belongs to some other space \(W_j\), then \(f\) can be written as \(f=\sum a_k\psi_{j,k}\).
