The Sampling Formula

Def: A function is said to be band-limited if its Fourier transform is supported on a compact interval \([a,b]\).

Def: The \(\mathrm{sinc}\) function is defined by

\[\mathrm{sinc}(x)\equiv\left\{\begin{matrix}\frac{\sin(\pi x)}{\pi x} & x\neq 0 \\ 1 & x=0\end{matrix}\right..\]

Note

\[\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}e^{2\pi i x\gamma}d\gamma=\left.\frac{e^{2\pi i x\gamma}}{2\pi i x\gamma}\right|_{-1/2}^{1/2}\]

\[=\frac{e^{\pi ix}-e^{-\pi x}}{2\pi i x}=\frac{\sin(\pi x)}{\pi x}\]

\[\mathrm{sinc}(x).\]

Theorem 8.4 (Sampling Formula):

Suppose that \(f\) is continuous and in \(M(\mathbb{R})\) whose Fourier transform is supported on \([-1/2,1/2]\). Then

\[f(x)=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f(n)\mathrm{sinc}(x-n).\]

Proof:

Consider a 1-periodic extension of the Fourier transform \(\hat f\) to \(\mathbb{R}\).

\[(\hat f)^{\hat.}(n)=\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}\hat f(x)e^{-2\pi i x n}dx\]

\[=\int_\mathbb{R}\hat f(x)e^{i2\pi (-n)x}=f(-n)\]

by the inversion theorem. Here the notation \(g^{\hat.}\) denotes the Fourier coefficients of \(g\). The Fourier series of \(\hat f\) is \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f(-n)e^{2\pi i n x}\).

Claim: This Fourier series is absolutely and unif convergent.

Proof of claim: \(|f(x)|\leq\frac{A}{1+x^2}\) for all \(x\in\mathbb{R}\) due to moderate decrease. Thus

\[|f(-n)e^{2\pi i n x}|=|f(-n)|\leq\frac{A}{1+n^2}\]

and by the \(p\)-series \(\sum\frac{A}{1+n^2}\) is convergent. Then, by the Weierstrass \(M\)-test \(\sum f(-n)e^{2\pi i nx}\) converges uniformly (also absolutely). That is the family \(\{g_N\}\) defined by

\[g_N=\sum_{|n|\leq N}f(n)e^{-2\pi i n\gamma}\]

is uniformly convergent. Thus,

\[\hat f(\gamma)=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f(n)e^{-2\pi i n\gamma}.\]

Then, by the inversion theorem

\[f(x)=\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}\hat f(\gamma)e^{2\pi i x\gamma}d\gamma\]

\[=\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}\left(f(n)e^{2\pi i x\gamma}\right)e^{2\pi i x\gamma}d\gamma\]

\[=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f(n)\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}e^{2\pi i \gamma(x-n)}d\gamma=\star\]

Since the sum is uniformly convergent, the interchange of summation and integration is justified.

\[\star=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f(n)\mathrm{sinc}(x-n). \ \square\]

Remark: \(\hat f\) need not be 1-periodic, as long as \(\hat f\) is supported on an interval of length \(L\), a similar calculation can be done.

Example 8.4.3 \(f\)-cont, \(f\in M(\mathbb{R})\), \(\mathrm{supp}\hat f\subset[-L/2,L/2]\). Then

\[f(x)=\sum_{n\in\mathbb{Z}}f\left(\frac{n}{L}\right)\mathrm{sinc}\left(\frac{x-n}{L}\right).\]

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:

Suppose that \(f\in S(\mathbb{R})\) and \(\|f\|=1\). Then

\[\left(\int_\mathbb{R}x^2|f(x)|^2dx\right)\left(\int_\mathbb{R}\gamma^2|\hat f(\gamma)|^2d\gamma\right)\geq\frac{1}{16\pi^2}\]

Proof:

\[\frac{d}{dx}|f(x)|^2=\frac{d}{dx}f\bar f=f'\bar f+f\overline{f'}\]

\[=f'\bar f+\overline{\bar f f'}\]

\[=2\mathscr{R}(f'\bar f).\]

By hypothesis and integration by parts

\[1=\|f\|^2=\int_\mathbb{R}|f|^2dx=\left[x|f(x)|^2\right]_{-\infy}^\infty-\int_\mathbb{R}x\frac{d}{dx}|f|^2dx\]

\[=-\int_\mathbb{R}x2\mathscr{R}(f'\bar f)dx\leq2\int_\mathbb{R}|x||f'||f|dx=\star\]

since \(\mathscr R z\leq |z|\). Note

\[\left<|x||f|,|f'|\right>=\star/2.\]

Then, by Cauchy-Schwartz

\[1\leq2\|x|f(x)|\|\|f'(x)\|\]

and by Parsevals (\(\|\hat f\|=\|f\|\)) and \(f'\rightarrow 2\pi i\gamma\hat f(\gamma)\),

\[1\leq2\|x|f(x)|\|\|2\pi i\gamma\hat f\|\]
