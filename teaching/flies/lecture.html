Nature of the Interaction

  • flower has markings to help orient fly
  • flower has parts trying to place pollen of flies head
  • fly wants to get nectar from base of tube
  • fly proboscis does not roll up
    • has to drag it across the sandy terrain

Darwins Hawkmoth Prediction

  • … this is totally related to darwins hawkmoth prediction!
  • when european naturalists came across this orchid in madagascar
  • they were baffled by what would pollinate such an orchid
  • with its long nectar spur
  • darwin developed a coevolutionary argument to predict something like the hawkmoth
  • 30 years later the hawkmoth was finally observed by european naturalists
  • i say european because this stuff was likely known by first nations long ago

Revisiting the Data

flies reward longer flowers (mutual size-queens => selection for longer parts)

  • flies more likely to pollinate flowers with longer tubes
  • so both reward partners with longer parts
  • so the interaction selects for longer parts in both

A mutualistic arms race!
