\makelettertitle

I am applying for the postdoctoral position working on a NIH-funded research project developing statistical methods in population genetics. I believe my expertise, work ethic and research interests make me an ideal candidate for this position. In particular, my research has focused on formalizing and applying theoretical quantitative genetics to model and infer coevolution. My doctoral dissertation, which I successfully defended on June 30, 2020, contributes to this pursuit by providing a mechanistic derivation of a holistic framework capturing the stochastic eco-evolutionary dynamics of populations, the conditions for the maintenance of mutualisms in the face coevolutionary arms races, and a novel statistical method to infer coevolution in the wild. In each of these models, coevolutionary dynamics are derived from biological first principles using models of fitness as a function of phenotype. By interpreting the quantitative trait as location in continuous space, the underlying partial differential equations can be seen as stochastic generalizations of classical reaction-diffusion models used to study spatial ecology. Indeed, the original motivation for much of my work was to develop a spatial model of stochastic coevolutionary dynamics to infer the coevolutionary process across continuously distributed populations. Although the theoretical tools I have developed in my dissertation were derived from measure-valued branching processes, the results ignore the genealogical information provided by branching processes. Since this information is crucial for evolutionary inference, I have been eager to study spatially structured branching processes and their statistical application in more detail. Hence, I believe working with you would provide me with an ideal opportunity to broaden my scope of scientific expertise. By hiring me as a postdoctoral researcher, you will gain a very enthusiastic, curious and productive new lab member who is dedicated to maintaining an inclusive and safe space for all. Taken together, I believe this position provides the ideal next step in my career as a scientist. I hope that you agree.
