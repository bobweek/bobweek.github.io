Variance response to genetic drift

Bob Week

5/8/2019

Given our distribution of traits

\[\phi(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi G}}\exp\left(-\frac{(x-\bar x)^2}{2G}\right)\]

we can calculate the distribution of sample moments. In particular, given a sample \(N_1,\dots,N_I\), we have

\[\bar X\equiv\frac{1}{I}\sum_iX_i\sim\mathcal{N}(\bar x, G/I)\]

and

\[\hat G\equiv\frac{1}{I}\sum_i(X_i-\bar X)^2\sim G\chi^2_{I-1}.\]

This implies \(\mathbb{E}\hat G=G\frac{I-1}{I}\) and \(\mathbb{V}\hat G=2G\frac{I-1}{I^2}\). Taking inspiration from these results, we wish to derive the evolution of \(G\) under genetic drift and mutation. Mutation can be captured by

\[\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}=\frac{\mu I}{2}\frac{\partial^2\phi}{\partial x^2}\]

which is just the heat equation. Since we assume \(\phi\) is normal with the above expression, this implies

\[\frac{dG}{dt}=\mu I.\]

We’ll superimpose the effects of drift onto this base result. To do so in continuous time, we search for a natural diffusion process \(Y_t\) such that \(Y_t\) is \(\chi^2\)-distributed for each fixed \(t\). The CIR model provides this as a special case and, in general, can be written

\[dG=a(b-G)dt+\sigma\sqrt{G}d\xi\]

for a standard Brownian motion \(\xi\). A well estiblished result is, setting \(c=\frac{2a}{(1-e^{-a})\sigma^2}\),

\[\mathbb{E}\left(\int_t^{t+1}dG\right)=\frac{\frac{4ab}{\sigma^2}+2cGe^{-a}}{2c}=2b(1-e^{-a})+Ge^{-a}\]

\[\mathbb{V}\left(\int_t^{t+1}dG\right)=\frac{2\left(\frac{4ab}{\sigma^2}+4cGe^{-a}\right)}{(2c)^2}=\frac{2b}{c}(1-e^{-a})+2G\frac{e^{-a}}{c}.\]

Returning to our results from above, by setting \(\mathbb{E}\hat G=\mathbb{E}\left(\int_t^{t+1}dG\right)\) and \(\mathbb{V}\hat G=\mathbb{V}\left(\int_t^{t+1}dG\right)\) we find

\[b=0, \ \ a=\ln I-\ln(I-1), \ \ \sigma^2=2[\ln I-\ln(I-1)].\]

Hence, with mutation, the SDE for \(G\) becomes

\[dG=\Big(\mu I-[\ln I-\ln(I-1)]G\Big)dt+\sqrt{2[\ln I-\ln(I-1)]G}d\xi.\]
