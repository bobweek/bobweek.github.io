The stuff

Assumptions:

  • Two interacting guilds of species (e.g. plants and fly-pollinators).
  • A continuous trait mediates the interaction in both guilds (e.g. corolla and tongue lengths).
  • Individuals have a preference with whom they interact and this preference is trait based (e.g. we can assume the flys choose the flowers whose corolla depth would maximize their fitness on average but with some variance).

\[W_{fly}(x)=\int\frac{\iota_{fly}(x,y)\phi_{flower}(y)}{\int\iota_{fly}(x,y)\phi_{flower}(y)dy}W(x,y)dy\]

We could assume the distributions \(\phi_i\) are over all the individuals within guild \(i\).
